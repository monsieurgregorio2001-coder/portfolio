Scrum Sprint Plan & Work Breakdown Structure

UniversalHire - 120-Day Agile Execution Plan

Overview

Project Duration: 120 days (17 weeks)
Sprint Duration: 2 weeks
Total Sprints: 8.5 sprints (Sprint 0-8)
Team Size: 4 people
Methodology: Scrum with Kanban elements

Joins: Sprint 4

Sprint Structure (2-Week Sprints)

Sprint Ceremonies

Sprint Planning (Monday, Week 1) - 2 hours

  • Review product backlog
  • Select stories for sprint
  • Estimate story points (Fibonacci: 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13)
  • Define sprint goal
  • Break stories into tasks

Daily Standup (Daily, 15 min) - Async

  • What did the team do yesterday?
  • What will the team do today?
  • Any blockers?
  • Post in Slack/Discord

Sprint Review (Friday, Week 2) - 1 hour

  • Demo completed work to founder
  • Get feedback
  • Update product backlog based on feedback

Sprint Retrospective (Friday, Week 2) - 1 hour

  • What went well?
  • What didn’t go well?
  • What should we improve?
  • Action items for next sprint

Story Point Estimation Guide

Story Points Description Examples
1 Trivial task, < 2 hours Update text, fix typo, small UI tweak
2 Small task, half day Add new field, simple validation
3 Small feature, 1 day Create simple CRUD page
5 Medium feature, 2-3 days Implement search with filters
8 Large feature, 3-5 days Build pipeline drag-and-drop
13 Very large, 1 week Full email integration with OAuth
21+ Epic, break down Split into smaller stories

Team Velocity Target: 30-40 points per sprint (with full team of 4)

Definition of Ready (DoR)

Before a story enters a sprint, it must have:

  • Clear acceptance criteria
  • Estimated story points
  • Dependencies identified
  • Design mockups (if UI work)
  • Technical approach discussed
  • No blockers

Definition of Done (DoD)

A story is “Done” when:

  • Code complete and reviewed
  • Unit tests written and passing
  • E2E tests for critical paths
  • Documentation updated
  • Deployed to staging
  • Tested by Product Owner
  • Passes CI/CD pipeline
  • No known bugs

Backlog Management

Backlog Grooming: Every Wednesday, 1 hour

  • Refine upcoming stories
  • Estimate new stories
  • Prioritize based on founder feedback
  • Break down epics

Prioritization Framework:

  1. Must Have (MVP blockers)
  2. Should Have (Important but not critical)
  3. Could Have (Nice to have)
  4. Won’t Have (Future/Parking lot)
